## Top 10 Essential Product Management Skills

### 1. Prioritizing Roadmap

**Why it matters**: The most covered skill across Lenny's podcast. Every PM must decide what to build next.

**When to use**: 
- Planning quarterly roadmaps
- Evaluating competing feature requests
- Aligning team resources with business goals

**Key frameworks mentioned**:
- RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort)
- Shreyas Doshi's LNO Framework (Leverage, Neutral, Overhead)
- Opportunity cost vs. ROI thinking

**Guest insights**:
- Shreyas Doshi emphasizes that most execution problems are actually strategy problems
- Focus on leverage activities that create disproportionate impact

---

### 2. Measuring Product-Market Fit

**Why it matters**: Foundational growth skill that determines whether your product solves a real problem.

**When to use**:
- Post-launch validation
- Before scaling growth efforts
- When considering pivot decisions

**Key metrics**:
- Sean Ellis test (40% "very disappointed" threshold)
- Retention curves (flattening = PMF)
- NPS and user engagement signals

**Guest insights**:
- Elena Verna: Retention is the foundation of sustainable growth
- Start with PMF before optimizing growth loops

---

### 3. Defining Product Vision

**Why it matters**: Sets the long-term direction and inspires teams.

**When to use**:
- Starting a new product or feature area
- Rallying cross-functional teams
- Communicating strategy to stakeholders

**Best practices**:
- Make it inspirational but achievable
- Connect vision to user problems
- Use storytelling to bring it to life

**Guest insights**:
- Brian Chesky: Stay in "founder mode" and deeply involved in product details
- Marty Cagan: Empowered teams need a clear vision to operate autonomously

---

### 4. Positioning & Messaging

**Why it matters**: How you position your product determines market perception and competitive advantage.

**When to use**:
- Product launches
- Entering new markets
- Differentiating from competitors

**April Dunford framework**:
1. Identify your competitive alternatives
2. Isolate your unique capabilities
3. Map capabilities to value themes
4. Define your market category
5. Test with customers

**Guest insights**:
- April Dunford (Top 10 most downloaded episode): Positioning is context-setting for your product's value
- Bad positioning makes every other marketing activity harder

---

### 5. Writing North Star Metrics

**Why it matters**: Aligns entire organization around a single measure of customer value.

**When to use**:
- Setting company/team OKRs
- Designing growth experiments
- Making trade-off decisions

**Characteristics of good North Star**:
- Expresses customer value delivered
- Measures progress toward vision
- Actionable by teams
- Leading indicator, not lagging

**Examples**:
- Airbnb: Nights booked
- Slack: Messages sent by teams
- Spotify: Time spent listening

---

### 6. Product Discovery (Continuous)

**Why it matters**: Reduces risk of building the wrong thing.

**When to use**:
- Before committing to build
- Throughout product development
- When validating assumptions

**Teresa Torres approach**:
- Weekly customer touchpoints (minimum)
- Opportunity solution trees
- Test assumptions, not just validate ideas

**Guest insights**:
- Teresa Torres: Discovery and delivery should happen in parallel
- Focus on understanding problems, not collecting feature requests

---

### 7. Decision-Making Frameworks

**Why it matters**: PMs make dozens of decisions daily. Frameworks create consistency and speed.

**When to use**:
- Evaluating trade-offs
- Choosing between competing priorities
- Communicating rationale to stakeholders

**Key frameworks**:
- **LNO** (Leverage, Neutral, Overhead) - Shreyas Doshi
- **Pre-mortems** - Anticipate failure modes before starting
- **One-way vs. two-way doors** - Jeff Bezos framework
- **ICE scoring** (Impact, Confidence, Ease)

**Guest insights**:
- Shreyas Doshi: Stop optimizing everything; focus on leverage
- Most PMs over-index on execution, under-index on strategy

---

### 8. Designing Growth Loops

**Why it matters**: Sustainable growth comes from self-reinforcing systems, not tactics.

**When to use**:
- Scaling user acquisition
- Building retention mechanisms
- Creating network effects

**Types of growth loops**:
- Viral loops (users invite users)
- Content loops (UGC attracts new users)
- Paid loops (revenue â†’ marketing)

**Guest insights**:
- Elena Verna: PLG requires product-first thinking in every function
- Casey Winters: Understand your core loop before optimizing tactics

---

### 9. User Onboarding

**Why it matters**: First impression determines if users experience your product's core value.

**When to use**:
- Reducing activation drop-off
- Improving time-to-value
- Increasing trial-to-paid conversion

**Best practices**:
- Show value before asking for effort
- Reduce friction in critical path
- Personalize based on user intent
- Measure activation cohorts

**Guest insights**:
- Focus on "aha moment" delivery, not feature tours
- Elena Verna: Freemium forces you to perfect onboarding

---

### 10. Managing Up & Stakeholder Communication

**Why it matters**: PM success depends on influence without authority.

**When to use**:
- Weekly 1:1s with leadership
- Cross-functional alignment
- Navigating organizational politics

**Best practices**:
- Share context, not just status
- Surface problems early with proposed solutions
- Understand your manager's priorities and success metrics
- Build trust through consistent delivery

**Guest insights**:
- Shishir Mehrotra's PSHE framework for career growth
- Ethan Evans: Managing up is about making your manager successful
